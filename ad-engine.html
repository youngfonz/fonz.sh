Project Brief

Ad Engine — Partner Brief

A weekly ad-ops tool for ecommerce clients. What it is, where we are, and what's left to build.

What it is

A weekly ad-ops tool we run for ecommerce clients. Every Monday it:

  1. Pulls the client's Shopify products + reviews + recent orders
  2. Pulls last week's Meta Ads performance
  3. Asks Claude to write 5 fresh Instagram video ad scripts based on what's selling and what customers are saying
  4. Renders each script into a 15–30s MP4 via HeyGen (AI avatar talking head)
  5. We review and upload approved videos to the client's Meta Ads Manager
  6. The next week, it pulls performance for those new ads and emits an HTML report we send the client
The pitch to the client: "5 fresh ads a week, every week, with a performance report. You don't have to think about creative."

Where we are right now

The backend is built and tested — all the data pipelines work, the AI integrations work, the report renders cleanly. It currently runs as a command-line tool, which is fine for me to operate but not something we can hand to a client or an account manager.

Next step is wrapping it in a simple web app so the workflow is "click three buttons and review the output," not "type commands into a terminal."

What we need from each client (one-time, ~30 min)

  • A Shopify access token (they generate it in their store admin in ~5 min — they don't share their login)
  • An invite to their Meta Business Manager so we can pull ad performance and upload creatives
  • Their reviews platform API token (most likely Judge.me, but worth confirming)
  • Brand description, tone guidelines, things to avoid
  • Either a stock AI avatar pick or a 2-min video of their spokesperson if they want a custom one

What we set up on our side

  • Anthropic account (Claude API) — ~$1–2/month per client in usage
  • HeyGen account — ~$50–100/month per client for video render minutes
  • Meta Developer App — free, one-time setup, takes a few days for app review
  • Hosting — Vercel + Postgres for the web app, ~$20–50/month total

So our marginal cost per client is roughly $50–150/month in API/render fees, plus our time.

What's left to build

About 2–3 days of focused work:

  • Web app GUI so account managers (or the client themselves) can trigger pulls, review generated ads, paste in Meta ad IDs after uploading, and view the weekly report
  • Three small backend bugs caught in code review (one bug would crash the report on a fresh install)
  • Migrate from local SQLite to hosted Postgres so it can run as a real web service
  • Customer onboarding doc with screenshots of where to click in Shopify/Meta/Judge.me

After that, onboarding a new client is "send them a one-page doc, get 3 tokens back, configure their HeyGen avatar in our admin, run first pull."

Open questions for us to decide

  • Pricing model for clients? The plan baked in "30% rev share + $5K monthly retainer" defaults, but we should sanity-check that.
  • Approval workflow? Who at the client signs off on ads before we push to Meta — them in the app, us over Slack/email, or auto-publish on a delay?
  • First test client? Plan was scoped for Secret Club. Are we still going there first, or pivoting?
  • Account-manager play vs. self-serve? Big difference in the GUI: managed service (we operate, they review) is simpler; self-serve (they do everything) is bigger product surface but scales better.
