Black Girls Code × YouTube Playables

Rhythm & Rescue

A music game where kids don't learn about music — they make it. And the learning happens because they did.

The Big Idea

YouTube selected Black Girls Code to build a game for YouTube Playables — their in-app gaming platform that reaches hundreds of millions of people. We're building Rhythm & Rescue, a music education game where you rescue music from a villain called the Wizard of Static.

The whole game is built around one idea: you learn by doing, not by being taught. Kids don't sit through a lesson and then get to play. They play first. The music teaches them while they're making it.

happens because they did."

Meet the Characters

🎤
Nova
Young Black girl producer. Our hero. BGC's owned IP.

Nova is a young Black girl who makes music. She's not a sidekick. She's not rescued by someone else. She is the creator. When the Wizard of Static erases all music from the world, Nova goes section by section to bring it back — using her skills as a producer. She looks like the kids playing this game. That's intentional and it matters.

Nova doesn't belong to YouTube. Nova belongs to BGC. She's a character we build once and use across everything we make — events, curriculum, media, future games.

🤖
ECHO
Nova's companion. Scripted AI character (not live AI).

ECHO is a companion who lives inside the game world and quietly teaches kids what AI actually is — a tool that helps, not something that replaces you. ECHO is scripted. Pre-written lines. No live AI, no generation. This is a deliberate design choice: it keeps the game safe for kids and keeps the focus on Nova as the creator.

ECHO never solves anything for Nova. ECHO echoes — asks questions, names what Nova just did, celebrates the choice she made. The player is always the one making music.

The Story

The Wizard of Static has erased all music. The world has gone gray and silent — no rhythm, no melody, no voice. Nova enters five broken sections of this world: the Rhythm Ruins, the Groove River, the Chord Fields, the Melody Heights, and the Static Citadel. In each section, she finds one missing piece of music, completes a challenge to rescue it, and adds it to the song she's building. When all five pieces are back, she performs the complete song — and music, color, and life flood back into the world.

How You Play — The 5 Sections

Each section gives you one new piece of the song. At the end, you put all five together and perform it. Here's what happens in each one:

1
Section 1 — Rhythm Ruins
The Pulse

You tap falling tiles in time to a drum beat — like a rhythm game. Four lanes: kick, snare, kick, snare. The world is completely silent when you arrive. Every tap you hit makes a drum sound. By the end of the section, you've performed the drum pattern and rescued it. The world gets its first heartbeat.

🔓 Drums unlocked — world starts to pulse
2
Section 2 — Groove River
The Groove

You build a bassline on an 8-step grid. The drums are playing underneath you. You tap which beats you want the bass to hit. It loops back in real time so you hear what you're making. You can't go wrong — every note is in key. When you've filled enough steps, ECHO plays the full bass stem on top of yours. World gets depth.

🔓 Bass unlocked — world gets foundations
3
Section 3 — Chord Fields
The Color

You find three big buttons — Low, Mid, High — and press different combinations to make chords. Drums and bass are playing. You're feeling how different note combinations change the emotion of the music. When you land on a "resonant" chord, the world lights up with color. There's no wrong answer — you're just discovering what feels right.

🔓 Chords unlocked — world fills with color
4
Section 4 — Melody Heights
The Melody

Back to falling tiles — but now five lanes, each a different musical pitch (color-coded). You're playing the melody on the trumpet. Any tile you hit plays a real note. Hit the tiles in time and you're playing the actual song. Even tapping randomly sounds musical because every lane is in key. This is the part that gets stuck in your head.

🔓 Melody unlocked — world comes to life
5
Section 5 — Static Citadel
The Voice

The Wizard scattered the lyrics across the citadel. Word tiles float across the screen — fragments of the hook. You tap them in order to rebuild the lyric. Each word you tap plays that syllable of the vocal. When you assemble the last word, the full song plays for the very first time — all five layers together. This is the first time you've heard the complete track.

🔓 Vocals unlocked — full song plays for the first time

The Finale

🎤

Nova Takes the Stage

All five layers appear in the Beat Builder — drums, bass, chords, melody, vocals. You can mute and unmute each one and hear how they stack. Then you tap Perform. The full song plays. The Static dissolves. Color floods back into every corner of the world. Nova walks to center stage. The music is back.

ECHO's final line:
"I only echoed what was already in you.
You rescued the music. But you made it yours."

After the credits — Studio Mode unlocks. Free-play with all five instruments, all sounds, no instructions. Make whatever you want. This is the reward for finishing: creative freedom you earned.

The One Thing to Remember

This game has no Game Over. You cannot fail. You cannot lose. Every section is designed so that any kid, at any skill level, completes it and moves forward. Challenge is opt-in — there's a score if you want one — but progress never stops. We believe kids learn more when they're not afraid of getting it wrong. The whole game is designed around that belief.
